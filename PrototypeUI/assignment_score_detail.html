Citation


○Paragraph(s) lack citation OR citation is irrelevant to the established claims in the topic and thesis statements.

○Paragraph(s) uses a relevant citation to the topic and thesis statements but are unsuccessful in the execution of the citation, e.g. "dropped abruptly" into the writing.

○Citations are clearly linked to the words around them, usually with a signal phrase

○Citations are so superbly selected and executed that it enhances the overall argument.


Analysis


○Paragraph(s) do not provide analysis of the citation that explains the established topic and thesis statements; summary

○The "analysis" in the paragraph(s) attempts to explain the established topic and thesis statements but there is a misunderstanding of the citation.

○The analysis provides some insight but tends to be superifical in the writer's understanding and/or interpretation of the evidence.

○Analysis is deep and insightful, making logical and appropriate conclusions from the presented evidence.

Thesis Statement

○Writing does not have a thesis statement that addresses the prompt or establishes the writer's position.

○Writing has a thesis statement that addresses the prompt but the statement is either incomplete, incorrect, or the statement does not clearly show the writer's position.

○Writing has a thesis statement that completely and accurately addresses the prompt and that clearly establishes the writer's position.

○Thesis statement is clearly linked with the introductory paragraph and is flawless in its execution (e.g. using appropriate grammatical structures).

Topic Sentence

○Paragraph(s) lack a relevant topic sentence that answers the prompt.

○Paragraph(s) have a topic sentence that answers the prompt but does not clearly show the writer's position.

○Topic sentence introduces a specific element relevant to the writer's position on the prompt.

○The specific element introduced in the topic sentence is specific enough to link to the rest of the paragraph.

Writing Mechanics

○Writing has up to four cumulated errors.

○Writing has up to two cumulative errors

○Writing has no errors.
